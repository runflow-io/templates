Why templates

Generalist models are great at demos.
Specialists are what actually ships.

One model that promises to do everything will frustrate you the moment you try to use it for one specific job. Runflow templates wrap a chain of specialist models around the exact task you're doing.

Generalist prompt-and-pray

  • Closed-source models can't accept masks, so you can't point at one object and edit only that object
  • Quality collapses the moment you chain more than one edit on the same image
  • They add artifacts (extra props, distorted edges, hallucinated lighting) you never asked for
  • "Make a lifestyle product photo" returns wrong angle, wrong lighting, wrong category
  • One prompt fits all, so 2 out of 10 attempts are usable and you are the QA loop

Runflow specialist template

  • Pixel-precise masks: point at one object, edit only that object, leave the rest of the image untouched
  • Every step is a separate specialist call, so chaining ten edits doesn't compound drift
  • Specialist models keep the source pixels intact, no artifacts you didn't ask for
  • gpt-4o reads the product first, picks scene prompts by category, then runflow/product-isolation does the cutout
  • runflow/smart-resize and outpaint produce every ratio, pixel-accurate, on your own infra
